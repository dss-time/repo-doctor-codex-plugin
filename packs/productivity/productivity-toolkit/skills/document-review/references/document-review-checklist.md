# Document Review Checklist

- Purpose, audience, scope, and required decision are clear.
- Headings form a coherent hierarchy and each section earns its place.
- Claims have evidence; quotations and citations resolve to a source.
- Figures retain units, periods, definitions, and consistent precision.
- Terms, names, versions, dates, and cross-references are consistent.
- Tables and figures have labels, captions, source notes, and readable structure.
- Links, footnotes, numbering, and internal references are valid when tools permit checking.
- Recommendations identify rationale, owner only when known, timing only when known, and dependencies.
